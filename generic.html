SAFE HARBOR PROJECT

This project focused on creating a welcoming space for individuals experiencing primary homelessness, particularly intermittent rough sleepers. It aimed to provide a safe and supportive environment, enhancing the spatial experience while addressing the users' specific needs and balancing opportunities and constraints.

CREATE CONNECTION |

Large windows overlooking the outdoors and connecting interior spaces help foster a sense of openness. A community kitchen and garden further enhance this connection by allowing neighboring residents and communities to engage with the homeless, promoting interaction and support through shared spaces.

ALLOW FOR PRIVATE AND PUBLIC INTERACTION |

The design features custom beds that can be adjusted for both private and public use. It also includes a community hallway where users can leave personal messages and spaces that encourage public interaction, ensuring a thoughtful balance between privacy and community engagement.
