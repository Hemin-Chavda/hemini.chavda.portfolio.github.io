WORKPLACE DESIGN

This project focused on redesigning Level 4 of Curtin Architecture Building 418, home to Curtin's Design and Built Environment staff and researchers. Located on Nyungar land, the aim was to create a space that reconnects with Aboriginal culture. Inspired by the "Djilba Season," a transitional period between cold and warm, I used local sustainable materials and custom furniture that reflect cultural significance while being functional.

ABORIGINAL DESIGN |

The design features abstract representations of Aboriginal symbols, integrating them subtly into the space to respectfully acknowledge Aboriginal art.

DJILBA SEASON |

The design incorporated elements from the Djilba Season, characterized by its transitional weather and renewal themes, and abstract Aboriginal symbols. These symbols were subtly integrated into the space to honor cultural heritage while maintaining a modern aesthetic. This approach aimed to create a meaningful connection to the local environment.

This project provided valuable insight into Aboriginal design and how it can be thoughtfully incorporated into a workplace setting. I learned that Aboriginal design cannot be translated literally, but rather subtly referenced in ways that honor the culture. It was a rewarding experience to explore the Djilba season and gain a deeper understanding of its cultural significance.
